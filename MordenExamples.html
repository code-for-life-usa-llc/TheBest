Modern Examples of Racial Discrimination

Anti-Asian Racism During COVID-19 Pandemic

Asians, especially East Asians, were blamed for the coronavirus, leading to a global rise in hate crimes—verbal harassment, physical assaults, and even deadly attacks.

  • In 2021, a man killed six Asian women during a mass shooting at Atlanta spas.
  • Asian-owned businesses saw a sharp drop in customers due to fear and prejudice, not health data.
  • Phrases like “China virus” or “kung flu” used by public figures helped fuel racism and hate.
George Floyd and Systemic Anti-Black Racism (2020)

George Floyd, a Black man, was murdered by a Minneapolis police officer who kneeled on his neck for over 9 minutes, despite Floyd being unarmed and pleading for his life.

  • His death sparked massive protests (Black Lives Matter) worldwide, highlighting systemic racism in policing.
  • His case is one of many—others include Breonna Taylor, Elijah McClain, and Eric Garner.
Latino Discrimination in Immigration Enforcement

Some quick example text to build on the card title and make up the bulk of the card’s content.

  • U.S. immigration policy under the Trump administration led to thousands of Latino children being separated from their parents.
  • Latino workers have been disproportionately targeted in workplace immigration raids, sometimes with questionable legal backing.
  • Immigrants described as "invaders" or "criminals" has fueled xenophobia.
Islamophobia Post-9/11 and Ongoing

The 9/11 terrorist attacks triggered a dramatic surge in anti-Muslim sentiment worldwide, particularly in the U.S. and Europe.

  • In the weeks after 9/11, hate crimes against Muslims and those perceived as Muslim skyrocketed in the U.S. Muslim women wearing hijabs reported being spat on, harassed, or attacked.
  • Muslims have often been portrayed in media as terrorists, villains, or extremists, reinforcing negative stereotypes.
